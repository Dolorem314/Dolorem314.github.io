Optogenetics Showcase

What is Optogenetics?
Optogenetics is a technique that involves the use of light to control and manipulate genetically modified cells or organisms. It allows researchers to selectively activate or inhibit specific cells or neural circuits with high precision.
Applications of Optogenetics
Optogenetics has a wide range of applications in neuroscience research, including:
  • Studying neural circuits and their functions
  • Investigating the role of specific neurons in behavior
  • Treating neurological disorders
  • Controlling cellular activity in vitro
Advantages of Optogenetics
Optogenetics offers several advantages over traditional methods:
  • Precise control over cell activation or inhibition
  • Ability to target specific cell types or circuits
  • Non-invasive and reversible
  • High temporal resolution
